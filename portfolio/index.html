Hi, I'm Daniel, this page details my journey in sharing my ideas through technology, design and art.

Press a key on your keyboard, some of the crosses to the right will stop, they represent cars. I built the simulation to show a friend how his driving style affects a larger network. More on Traffic.

Right now I'm working on Google Calendar. Whilst Traffic changed the minds of a few friends, working on Calendar lets me help hundreds of millions of users. More on Calendar.

Being able to make things and share them with the world is something that I have always found deeply inspiring. In my undergraduate degree, I led a team of students building a web app to share life streaming videos online. More on Voio.

As the web progresses, we are using it to share more and more information. For my undergraduate dissertation, I worked on a scalable server to handle the quantity of data from internet of things devices. More on Transport Data Web Server.

Sharing things on the web is really about sharing experiences, ideas and emotions. In efforts toward this, I have played with a variety of media including posters, magazines and clothing as well as more abstract forms such as lighting. More on Graphic Design.

For me personally I have found a lot of enjoyment in photography, shaping the world into interesting, beautiful and graphic imagery. More on Photography.

Painting and Drawing has provided me with even more flexibility to convey ideas. Highlighted here is a project built to convey the feeling of finding a new world in the pages of a book. More on In a Hole in the Ground.
